# Block 5 — What Is Bitcoin? Content Plan

## Goal

Turn the relevant My First Bitcoin curriculum content in this block into a practical, interactive learning app.

## Canonical practical direction

“Bitcoin is not…” misconception game, sound money property comparison.

## Source curriculum copied into this block

- `curriculum-content/5-what-is-bitcoin/0-the-creation-of-bitcoin.md`
- `curriculum-content/5-what-is-bitcoin/1-how-does-bitcoin-work.md`
- `curriculum-content/5-what-is-bitcoin/2-bitcoin-as-sound-digital-money.md`
- `curriculum-content/11-back-matter/3-key-concepts.md`
- `curriculum-content/11-back-matter/4-glossary.md`

## Key concepts to preserve

- Bitcoin origin
- Peer-to-peer digital cash
- Fixed supply
- Network and transactions
- Bitcoin as sound digital money
- Key terms and misconceptions

## Practical app thesis

Misconception sorter plus sound-money comparison where learners separate what Bitcoin is, is not, and why its properties matter.

## Build-ready structure

Each block app should use this structure:

1. **Learn** — short explanation adapted from the copied curriculum content.
2. **Try** — the practical game/simulator for the block.
3. **Discuss** — prompts for facilitators or groups.
4. **Apply** — real-world scenario or decision.
5. **Build** — printable/classroom extension.
