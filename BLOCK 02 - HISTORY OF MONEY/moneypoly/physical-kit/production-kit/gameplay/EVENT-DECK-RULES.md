# Event Deck Rules

## Deck structure
Build **6 separate decks** with **20 cards each**:
- 20 Barter cards
- 20 Commodity Money cards
- 20 Gold cards
- 20 Credit & Ledgers cards
- 20 Fiat cards
- 20 Bitcoin cards

Total: **120 event cards**.

## How to use them
- use the deck that matches the current era
- draw 1 event per table pair at each major transition or every 3 trade attempts
- banker applies the effect immediately
- event master keeps the room roughly synchronized

## Operating rule
Do not try to use all 20 cards in a single session. The larger deck gives variety and replayability.

## Recommended draw count by format
### 60-minute workshop
- Barter: 2 draws
- Commodity Money: 2 draws
- Gold: 1-2 draws
- Credit & Ledgers: 1-2 draws
- Fiat: 2 draws
- Bitcoin: 2 draws

### 90-minute workshop
- Barter: 3 draws
- Commodity Money: 3 draws
- Gold: 2-3 draws
- Credit & Ledgers: 2-3 draws
- Fiat: 3 draws
- Bitcoin: 3 draws

## What each deck should teach
### Barter
- double coincidence of wants
- perishability
- indivisibility
- local scarcity
- the difficulty of saving for the future

### Commodity Money
- saleability
- acceptance effects
- competing monies
- inflation from new supply
- standardization

### Gold
- strong savings properties
- portability friction
- verification
- clipping / debasement risk
- seizure / transport risk

### Credit & Ledgers
- trust in issuers
- signatures and settlement
- reserve pressure
- branch mismatch
- institutional dependence

### Fiat
- money printing
- Cantillon effects
- price distortion
- freezes / restrictions
- nominal vs real wealth

### Bitcoin
- fixed supply
- self-custody responsibility
- hot vs cold tradeoffs
- verification
- resilience against monetary debasement
