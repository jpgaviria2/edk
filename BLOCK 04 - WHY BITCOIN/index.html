Block 4

Why Bitcoin?

Bitcoin makes more sense after the problems are clear: inflation, centralized control, censorship, counterparty risk, and dependence on trusted intermediaries.

Start lessonActivities
Bridge to Bitcoin

Bitcoin is not introduced as magic. It is introduced as a response.

Learners match the pain points of fiat systems to Bitcoin properties, then discuss the new responsibilities Bitcoin creates.

Web lesson

Connect each monetary problem to a Bitcoin design response.

1

Problem: supply can expand

When money can be created at political or institutional discretion, savers face the risk that their stored value is diluted over time.

2

Response: fixed issuance schedule

Bitcoin has a transparent supply cap and issuance rules that users can verify. The point is not trust in a promise; the point is rules enforced by the network.

3

Problem: payments need permission

Banks, apps, and payment processors can block, reverse, delay, or surveil transactions. That creates dependency on intermediaries.

4

Response: peer-to-peer settlement

Bitcoin lets people send value directly across the network. The system still has tradeoffs, but the base design reduces reliance on a central payment gatekeeper.

5

Problem: custody risk

If someone else controls the keys or account, they can freeze access, lose funds, or change terms. Convenience often comes with counterparty risk.

6

Response: self-custody and verification

Bitcoin allows users to hold keys and verify rules themselves. That creates freedom, but also responsibility for backups, privacy, and safe handling.

Problem → solution matcher

Pick the Bitcoin feature that responds to the problem.

Problem: a central authority can create more money and dilute savers.

Teach

Human story

Tell the story as a long search for digital cash: how do you make online money scarce, transferable, and hard to censor without putting one company in charge?

Apply

Scenario cards

Give groups real-world scenarios: inflation, frozen account, cross-border payment, bank failure, merchant chargeback. Ask which Bitcoin property matters and what responsibility comes with it.
