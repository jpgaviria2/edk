Why Bitcoin?

Bitcoin is easier to understand as a response to problems: inflation, censorship, counterparty risk, and centralized control.

StartHost this lesson
Learner progress

One block. One core idea. One next action.

Lesson path

Short, guided, and hard to get lost in.

1

Name the problem

Modern money and payments depend on institutions that can change rules, expand supply, or block access.

2

Match the design

Bitcoin responds with fixed supply, peer-to-peer settlement, self-custody, and user verification.

3

Accept the tradeoff

More control creates more responsibility: keys, backups, privacy, and careful use.
