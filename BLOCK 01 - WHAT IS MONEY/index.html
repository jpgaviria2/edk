Block 1

What Is Money?

Money is not just paper, coins, or numbers on a screen. It is a social tool that helps people trade, compare value, save for the future, and coordinate with strangers.

Web lesson

Discover the job money performs before asking whether any money is good.

1

Why money exists

Without money, every trade requires two people to want exactly what the other person has. Money reduces that friction by becoming the thing people are willing to accept even when they do not want the final good yet.

2

The three core functions

Money works as a medium of exchange, a unit of account, and a store of value. If one of those functions breaks, daily life gets harder: prices become confusing, saving becomes risky, and trade slows down.

3

The properties of good money

Good money is durable, portable, divisible, recognizable, fungible, scarce, and widely accepted. Different societies tested cattle, salt, shells, metals, paper, bank balances, and now digital money against those properties.

Play

Function classifier

Read a real-world moment and identify which money function is being used.

A café lists espresso at $3 and a sandwich at $9.

Teach

Candy, dollar, monopoly bill

Ask who would trade candy for a dollar, then who would trade candy for a monopoly bill. The discussion reveals that money depends on trust, acceptance, and future usefulness.

Apply

Score candidate monies

Have learners score five candidates: bananas, gold, dollars, gift cards, and Bitcoin. Which one works for a quick purchase? Which one works for long-term savings?
