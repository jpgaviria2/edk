Block 3

Fiat, Inflation & Purchasing Power

Fiat money works because people are required and willing to accept it, but its supply can expand. When more money chases the same goods, each unit can buy less.

Start lessonActivities
Beginner funnel

From sound money to money by decree.

This lesson shows how modern money depends on trust in institutions, central banks, and policy decisions — then lets learners feel the effects through prices and wages.

Web lesson

Make purchasing-power loss visible instead of abstract.

1

What fiat means

Fiat money is not redeemable for a fixed amount of gold or another commodity. It has value because law, habit, tax obligations, and social acceptance keep people using it.

2

Money supply matters

If the amount of money increases faster than the goods and services available, people can have more currency units while each unit buys less.

3

Prices are signals

Prices are not random numbers. They transmit information about scarcity, demand, and the value people place on goods. Inflation distorts those signals.

4

Wages can lag prices

A person can receive a raise and still become poorer if rent, food, energy, and other essentials rise faster than income.

5

New money enters unevenly

Freshly created money does not reach everyone at the same time. Those who receive it first can buy before prices fully adjust; those farthest away face higher prices later.

6

CBDCs extend fiat rails

Digital fiat can make payments faster, but it also raises questions about surveillance, programmability, exclusion, and control.

Learn · Play · Teach · Apply

Use it as a self-guided lesson or a live classroom block.

Play

Auction simulator prompt

One item, three bidders, then more money is added but the item supply stays the same.

Everyone has more dollars, but there is still only one bottle of water.

Teach

Run the two-round auction

Auction a snack with a fixed amount of classroom money. Then hand out extra money and auction the same kind of snack again. Compare prices and ask who benefited.

Apply

Budget pressure

Have learners choose a monthly budget, then raise rent, food, and transport prices. They must decide what gets cut when wages do not keep up.
